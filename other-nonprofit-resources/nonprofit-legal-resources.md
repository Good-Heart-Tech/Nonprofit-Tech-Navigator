# Nonprofit Legal Resources

Running a nonprofit takes more than passion—it requires a solid legal foundation. Having a lawyer review your bylaws, contracts, and policies can prevent costly mistakes, ensure compliance, clarify roles, and protect your mission from legal distractions that drain time and resources.

### Finding the Right Legal Support for Your Nonprofit

Nonprofits often worry about legal costs, but many lawyers are willing to help, especially if they support your mission. Here’s where to start:

* **Local Law Firms** – Reach out to firms with a history of working with nonprofits. Some attorneys offer discounted or pro bono services, especially if your mission aligns with their values.
* **Bar Association Pro Bono Programs** – Many state and local bar associations have programs that connect nonprofits with legal assistance.
* **Law School Clinics** – Universities often run legal clinics where law students (supervised by licensed attorneys) provide low-cost or free legal help.
* **Nonprofit Legal Centers** – Organizations like **Charitable Allies** ([**charitableallies.org**](https://charitableallies.org/)) specialize in affordable legal support for nonprofits, offering guidance on governance, contracts, compliance, and more.

### Don’t Wait Until There’s a Problem

Legal issues are easiest to handle before they arise. Make legal review a part of your nonprofit’s standard processes so you can operate confidently and securely. Whether you find a local firm that believes in your cause or use a service like [**Charitable Allies**](https://charitableallies.org/get-started/), taking proactive steps now can save your organization from future headaches.
