# Getting Started: A Tech Setup Roadmap for New Nonprofits

Setting up technology for a nonprofit can feel overwhelming, especially when you are wearing many hats. The good news: you do not have to do it all at once, and most of what you need is free or heavily discounted for nonprofits.

This page puts the most important steps in order. Work through them from top to bottom, and follow the links for detailed instructions. If you ever get stuck, [reach out to us](https://goodhearttech.org/contact/). We are happy to help.

## Step 1: Get your free email and productivity suite

Your email and documents are the foundation everything else builds on. Both Microsoft and Google offer their full productivity suites free to eligible nonprofits, so you do not need to pay for email, word processing, video calls, or file storage.

## Step 3: Protect your email domain

A few DNS records (SPF, DKIM, and DMARC) prove that email from your domain is really from you. They keep your messages out of spam folders and make it much harder for scammers to impersonate your organization.

## A few tips as you go

* Take it one step at a time. You do not have to finish everything in a day.
* Use an admin account for setup, and keep everyday work in a regular account.
* Save your passwords and recovery codes in a password manager, not a notebook or spreadsheet.
* When in doubt, [ask us](https://goodhearttech.org/contact/). Helping nonprofits with exactly this is what we do, for free.
