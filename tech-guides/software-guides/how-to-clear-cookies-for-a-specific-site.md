# How to Clear Cookies for a Specific Site

Cookies are small pieces of data that websites store in your browser to keep you signed in, remember preferences, and track activity; sometimes they become outdated or corrupted, causing login issues, loading errors, or stuck sessions, so clearing them can reset the site’s behavior and fix problems.

### **Google Chrome**

1. Open Chrome.
2. Go to the site you want to clear cookies for.
3. Click the **lock icon** (🔒) in the address bar.
4. Select **Cookies and site data** (or **Site settings** → **Clear data**).
5. Choose the domain → click **Remove**.

**Alternate path:**

* Menu (⋮) → **Settings** → **Privacy and security** → **Site settings** → **View permissions and data stored across sites**
* Search for the site → **Clear data**.

***

### **Microsoft Edge**

1. Open Edge.
2. Go to the site in question.
3. Click the **lock icon** (🔒) in the address bar.
4. Select **Cookies and site data** → remove the domain entries you need.

**Alternate path:**

* Menu (⋯) → **Settings** → **Cookies and site permissions** → **Manage and delete cookies and site data** → **See all cookies and site data**
* Search for the domain → **Delete**.
