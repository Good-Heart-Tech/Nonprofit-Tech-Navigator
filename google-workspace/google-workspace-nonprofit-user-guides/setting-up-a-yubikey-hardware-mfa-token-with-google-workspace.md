# Setting up a YubiKey (Hardware/MFA Token) with Google Workspace

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) adds an extra layer of security to your Google Workspace account. Using a hardware security key, such as a YubiKey key, provides a strong form of authentication that is phishing-resistant and highly secure. This guide walks you through the process of setting up a hardware security key for your Google Workspace account.

## **Step 1: Access Your Google Account Security Settings**

1. Sign in to your **Google Workspace** account.
2. Click your avatar (profile picture) in the **top right corner**, then select **"Manage your Google Account."**
3. In the left-hand menu, click **"Security."**

## **Step 2: Enable 2-Step Verification**

4. Under the **"How you sign in to Google"** section, find **"2-Step Verification"** and click **"Get started."**
5. If prompted, enter your password to continue.

## **Step 3: Add a Security Key**

6. Scroll down and click **"Passkeys and security keys."**
7.  Click **"+ Use a security key."**

    <figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (52).png" alt="" width="290"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

## **Step 4: Register Your Security Key**

8. Select **"USB or NFC"** and click **"Next."**
9. Insert your **hardware security key** into a USB port on your device.
10. When prompted, **touch the button** on your security key.
11. Follow any additional on-screen instructions to complete the setup.

## **Step 5: Confirm Setup**

12. Once registered, your security key will be listed as an authentication method.
13. You can now use it for **multi-factor authentication (MFA)** when signing into your Google Workspace account.
