# How to Apply for FREE Nonprofit Google Workspace Services

## :checkered\_flag: Prerequisites

* Nonprofit 501(c)(3) status, and the Letter of Exemption from the IRS.  We'll need to upload this with the application.  See [all eligibility requirements here](https://support.google.com/nonprofits/answer/3215869?hl=en).&#x20;
* This letter will also contain the organization's Employee ID Number (EIN)
* The organization’s physical address
* The organization’s online contact information
* Verification occurs through _Goodstack_

## :white\_check\_mark: Application Steps

{% hint style="info" %}
Google uses a third-party platform called [Goodstack ](https://goodstack.org/)for verifications.  During the application process, be sure to keep an eye out for communications from them.
{% endhint %}

#### Application Steps for Google for Nonprofits

1. Sign in with your Google account at: [https://www.google.com/nonprofits/](https://www.google.com/nonprofits/)
2. Click **Get started**.
3. Have a Gmail account and a phone number handy for the application. If you don't have one, create a create one using your cell number for verification. The number is only used for verification and is not retained long-term. Once the account is created:
   * Enable MFA using an authenticator app.&#x20;
   * Configure email forwarding to your primary email so you receive verification and status updates.
4. Complete each page of the application carefully.&#x20;
   * Upload the _IRS Letter of Exemption_ when prompted.
5. Add a representative from the nonprofit to the application in case [Goodstack](https://goodstack.org/) needs clarification or additional documentation.
6. Submit the application and wait for review.

Approval typically takes 2 to 14 days. If the application is declined, review the feedback, correct the issue, and reapply. There is no mandatory waiting period before submitting a revised application.

### :eyes: Checking Your Application Status

You can always check the status of your nonprofit application in [the Google Nonprofit Portal](https://www.google.com/nonprofits/account/).&#x20;

## :dollar: Converting From Paid Google Workspace to Free Google Workspace for Nonprofits

If you've already been paying for Google Workspace and want to move to free nonprofit licensing, you can’t convert directly from Business Standard, Business Plus, or Enterprise plans. [You must first be on **Business Starter** (or trial) to qualify.](https://support.google.com/a/answer/10069853)

First, confirm your org is verified at [google.com/nonprofits](https://www.google.com/nonprofits/account/u/0/orgs).

### If you have 10 or fewer users (easy)

* Downgrade your plan to **Business Starter** under **Billing > Subscriptions > Switch plan**.
* Submit this form: [Google for Nonprofits Licensing Conversion](https://support.google.com/nonprofits/contact/contact_us).
* In the issue field, write: _“Please convert all our licenses to Google for Nonprofit. We are already approved via the Nonprofit portal. Please let me know if you need any more information.”_

### If you have more than 10 users (planning required)

Google only allows **10 or fewer active users** on Business Starter. Here’s how to get under the limit safely. You'll likely want to schedule this over a weekend or at a time when users do not need access. Google support response times can vary.

1. **Suspend extra users**
   * In **Admin Console > Directory > Users**, suspend enough accounts to get down to 10 active licenses.
   * Suspended accounts retain usernames, passwords, and data until you reinstate them.
2. **Downgrade to Business Starter**
   * In **Billing > Subscriptions**, select **Switch plan > Business Starter**.
3. **Request nonprofit licensing conversion**
   * Submit the [conversion form](https://support.google.com/nonprofits/contact/contact_us) with this message: _“Please convert all our licenses to Google for Nonprofit. We are already approved via the Nonprofit portal. Please let me know if you need any more information.”_
   * It can take a couple of business days for Google support to respond and perform the upgrade. Be ready for downtime for your suspended users.&#x20;
4. **Reinstate users**
   * Once the nonprofit edition is active (up to 300 free licenses), reinstate the suspended users and assign them licenses.  Their data and settings will still be intact.

✅ End result: Your org moves to the free nonprofit plan with up to **300 licenses**.
