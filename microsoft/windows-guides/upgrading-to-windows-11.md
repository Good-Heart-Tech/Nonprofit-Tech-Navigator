# Upgrading to Windows 11

Microsoft is [ending support for Windows 10 in October 2025](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/windows-10-support-ends-on-october-14-2025-2ca8b313-1946-43d3-b55c-2b95b107f281). After that date, Windows 10 computers will no longer receive essential security updates, which makes them less safe to use. To keep your organization secure, it’s critical to move to Windows 11.

### Upgrading Your Current Computer

For devices managed by Good Heart Tech, you’ll see a desktop shortcut named **Windows11InstallationAssistant.exe** (you can also [download it manually here](https://www.microsoft.com/software-download/windows11)). The assistant will walk you through the upgrade step by step.

* **Admin rights required:** You must have administrative access to run the installer.
* **Compatibility check:** The installer will verify hardware requirements. If your device does not meet minimum standards, the assistant app will let you know.

### Replacing Incompatible Computers

If your device cannot run Windows 11, purchasing a replacement is highly recommended. When selecting a new system, we recommend:

* **Windows 11 Pro** ([never Home edition](https://teckpath.com/why-you-should-not-use-windows-10-11-home-for-business))
* **At least 16 GB of RAM**

Trusted purchase options include:

* [Dell Deals](https://deals.dell.com)
* [Dell Refurbished](https://www.dellrefurbished.com/)
* [TechSoup – nonprofit discounts](https://www.techsoup.org/search/products/dell/)
